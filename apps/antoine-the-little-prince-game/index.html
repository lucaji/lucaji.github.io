The Little Prince#

Everybody shall be well acquainted with the story of the Little Prince. I personally really like to learn languages, and having the same text available in more than one language, the dual-language books, for example, where the left page is in the original draft, and the right hand one, is in a foreign language.

I find this approach of being able to compare directly the two text - a Rosetta-Stone approach I would say - much more interesting and learning-prone.

So I decided to make the text of the story into a more lively, interactive and modern approach: putting it into a mobile app. Along with translations. I embarked in some google-research and found out some translations, of course that method is not 100% legal, as the translators do want to earn their bread, and simply not thinking about paying the fees might not be the best idea. Especially nowadays where everything is at-hand, just behind that “Search” textbox, I learned the hard way…

When I came to know that Antoine de Saint Exupery’s copyrights were becoming available onto the public domain I thrusted myself into making this translated book and I included a SpriteKit game as well as my first go at it. Things with copyrights are not as simple as clear water, so eventually I preferred to withdraw the apps from the stores (it had an Android companion app too).

This little app had the Little Prince’s story with translations in four different languages and included a shoot-em-all game with original music.

20 April 2016: Antoine has been pulled off the stores. My fatigue was nothing compared to professionals that actually do their work daily and want to get paid. As a matter of fact, I received only an alert of copyright infringement and that story simply ended up there - just like that. I have been lucky.
